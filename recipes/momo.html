Chicken Momo

Description

Momo, a cherished culinary gem hailing from Nepal, is a mouthwatering delight enjoyed both locally and across the globe. These intricately crafted dumplings encapsulate the essence of Nepalese cuisine. Carefully folded and filled with a tantalizing blend of minced meat, vegetables, and aromatic spices, momo showcases the culinary artistry passed down through generations. Steamed to perfection, these dumplings offer a tender, flavorful bite that harmonizes with a range of dipping sauces, creating a balanced and satisfying experience. Momo isn't just food; it's a communal tradition. Whether relished in street stalls, restaurants, or homes, momo gatherings foster connections and celebrate shared moments. This Nepalese treasure has transcended boundaries, captivating taste buds worldwide and embodying the warmth of cultural exchange.

Ingredients

Dough For Wrappers

  • 4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 tablespoon oil
  • water, as required
  • 1 pinch salt

Filling

  • 2 lbs lean ground meat (50% lamb or chicken & 50% pork works best)
  • 1 cup red onion, finely chopped
  • 1⁄2 cup green onion, finely chopped
  • 1 cup ripe tomatoes, finely chopped
  • 3 tablespoons fresh cilantro, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon fresh garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon fresh ginger, minced
  • 1⁄4 teaspoon nutmeg, freshly grated
  • 1⁄2 teaspoon turmeric
  • 1 tablespoon curry powder, or momo masala if available
  • 3 fresh red chilies, minced (or to taste)
  • 3 tablespoons cooking oil
  • salt and pepper

Directions

Steps for the Dough

  1. In a large bowl combine flour, oil, salt and water.
  2. Mix well, knead until the dough becomes homogeneous in texture, about 8-10 minute.
  3. Cover and let stand for at least 30 minute.

Steps for the Filling

  1. In a large bowl combine all filling ingredients.
  2. Mix well, adjust for seasoning with salt and pepper.
  3. Cover and refrigerate for at least an hour to allow all ingredients to impart their unique flavors.
  4. Give the dough a final knead.
  5. Prepare 1-in. dough balls.
  6. Take a ball, roll between your palms to spherical shape. Dust working board with dry flour.
  7. On the board gently flatten the ball with your palm to about 2-in circle.
  8. Make a few semi-flattened circles, cover with a bowl.
  9. Use a rolling pin to roll out each flattened circle into a wrapper.
  10. Continue until the wrapper attains 3-in diameter circular shape.
  11. Repeat with the remaining semi-flattened dough circles.
  12. For packing hold wrapper on one palm, put one tablespoon of filling mixture and with the other hand bring all edges together to the center, making the pleats.
  13. Pinch and twist the pleats to ensure the absolute closure of the stuffed dumpling.
  14. Heat up a steamer, oil the steamer rack well.
  15. Arrange uncooked dumplings in the steamer.
  16. Close the lid, and allow steaming until the dumplings are cooked through, about 10 minutes.
  17. Take dumplings off the steamer and serve immediately.
